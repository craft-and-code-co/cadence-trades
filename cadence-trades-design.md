## Problem Statement

Small trades and home service business owners (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, backflow testing, etc.) are stuck working IN their business instead of ON it. They lack the know-how, bandwidth, or tools to analyze their own performance data and make strategic decisions about pricing, hiring, marketing, and growth.

~80% do nothing — they check the bank account and hope. ~20% pay $1,500+/month for coaching programs that deliver generic, Tony Robbins-style motivation rather than data-driven, specific advice. No product exists that connects to a trades company's operational data and delivers specific, actionable coaching recommendations with projected dollar impact.

The founder is a trades business consultant who sees this pattern across multiple clients and has written a comprehensive 31K-word V1 spec.

## Demand Evidence

- Founder works directly with trades businesses as a consultant — firsthand observation across multiple clients, not hypothetical
- Helped target user Josh set up Jobber — deep relationship and understanding of the workflow
- Identified that ~20% of owners already pay $1,500+/month for coaching programs, proving willingness to pay for business guidance
- Coaching industry is rife with FTC warnings about scams and overcharging — pain is real and exploited
- No direct evidence of demand for a software product yet (pre-product stage). Assignment: validate with Josh and 2-3 other clients before building.

## Status Quo

Two paths exist today:

**Path 1 (~80%): Do nothing.** Owners lack the know-how or bandwidth to dig into their data. They run the business by gut feel, check the bank balance, and hope. They don't use the reporting features in their FSM software because they don't know what insights to look for.

**Path 2 (~20%): Expensive coaching programs.** Programs like BDR, The Contractor Fight, Built for the Trades, etc. charge $500-$2,000+/month. The founder describes these as "Tony Robbins style — designed to milk money." They deliver generic advice, require significant time commitment, and are not connected to the owner's actual business data. They sell motivation and community, not data-driven strategy.

**Cadence's wedge:** Be the thing that's more specific than a coach (because it reads your actual data) and more accessible than a consultant (because it costs a fraction and is available 24/7).

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target User: Josh**
- Owns a small backflow testing company
- 3 people total: Josh (does tests), one other tester, one back-office family member
- Uses Jobber (consultant helped set up) + QuickBooks
- Wins on low prices + speed + always answering the phone
- No advertising, no price evaluation, no competition evaluation, no membership program, no hiring plan
- Not tech savvy, but trying to get better with Jobber
- Classic "working IN the business" trap — wants to work ON it but has made himself irreplaceable
- Chicken-and-egg growth problem: can't grow without more people, can't hire without more revenue

**Narrowest Wedge:** A proactive email (SMS as a stretch goal pending validation) on Monday morning that says: "Your backflow test price is $25 below market. Raising it would add ~$1,050/month. Here's exactly what to say to your customers." + a conversational AI coach that can answer Josh's follow-up questions using his actual Jobber data.

The wedge is NOT a dashboard. It's a weekly email with a specific dollar figure and a specific action — with an app behind it for going deeper.

## Constraints

- Josh is not tech savvy — UX must be radically simple by default
- Data quality in Jobber will be messy — product must handle imperfect data gracefully
- Push notification infrastructure (email/SMS) is required from day one, not an afterthought
- AI responses must reference specific data points from Josh's business — generic advice destroys trust instantly
- Pricing must anchor to coaching replacement ($200-400/month), not SaaS tooling ($20-50/month)
- V1 must work with CSV/manual entry, but Jobber integration is an early priority

## Premises

1. **Push-first, app-second.** Push notifications (email/SMS) drive engagement with projected dollar impact. The app is the home base for going deeper — conversational, not chart-heavy.
2. **We replace the coach.** Competition is the $1,500/month coaching program, not FSM reporting. Price and position accordingly.
3. **CSV launches, Jobber follows fast.** CSV/manual entry for day one, but Jobber integration is an early priority to eliminate friction.
4. **Moat = knowledge applied to YOUR data.** Neither generic coaching knowledge nor raw analytics alone is defensible. The intersection — coaching intelligence applied to this specific company's numbers — is the moat.
5. **Progressive disclosure.** Radically simple default experience (briefing + coach chat). Dashboard depth available but never forced.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: AI Coach Chief of Staff (CHOSEN)
Strip the spec to its core: onboarding, Jobber/CSV data import, AI insight engine, and a conversational coach. Replace the dashboard-heavy design with a push-first "weekly briefing" model. The app is a coach you text, not a dashboard you check.

- **Effort:** M (4-6 weeks with AI-assisted development)
- **Risk:** Medium — requires validating push engagement works
- **Pros:** Matches real user behavior, smallest surface area, fastest to ship, coach-replacement pricing
- **Cons:** Power users may find it "thin," push infra needed from day one, AI quality must be excellent

### Approach B: Full Spec V1
Build the complete 31K-word spec — dashboard, insights page, campaign generator, coach chat, data sources, settings. Comprehensive but 8-12 weeks before first user validation.

- **Effort:** L (8-12 weeks)
- **Risk:** High — large surface area before any validation
- **Pros:** Comprehensive, demo-ready, serves both small and mid-size
- **Cons:** 8-12 weeks to first user, Josh won't use 70% of it

### Approach C: Consultant Multiplier
Build for consultants (like the founder) who serve multiple trades businesses. Multi-tenant from day one. Founder is own first customer.

- **Effort:** M (4-6 weeks)
- **Risk:** Medium — smaller TAM but instant validation
- **Pros:** Immediate self-validation, solves "Josh won't log in" problem
- **Cons:** Doesn't test core thesis (can owner use it alone?), may stay niche

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A: AI Coach Chief of Staff** — because it tests the core thesis (can an AI replace the human coach for Josh?) with the smallest possible build. If Josh engages with push-driven AI coaching, everything else layers on. If he doesn't, you learn in 4 weeks instead of 12.

### Core Screens (3 total):

1. **Weekly Briefing (Home):** "Morning, Josh." + 3 KPIs (revenue this week vs. last, jobs completed, average ticket price) + 1-2 insight cards with specific dollar projections and action CTAs. No charts, no navigation maze.

2. **Coach Chat:** Conversational interface where every AI response references Josh's actual data. RAG-powered with trades-specific coaching knowledge. Not generic advice — specific numbers from his Jobber/CSV data.

3. **Push Notifications (the real front door):** Monday morning email/SMS with dollar figures and specific actions. "You left $1,050 on the table last month. Here's a 2-minute plan to fix it." This is where engagement starts.

### What's IN scope for V1:
- Onboarding (simplified — trade, company size, pain points, goals)
- CSV import (required for launch) + Jobber OAuth integration (stretch for V1; required for V1.1)
- AI insight engine (generates specific recommendations with dollar projections — see Insight Engine Data Model below)
- Conversational coach (RAG-powered, data-aware)
- Push notifications (email, stretch: SMS)
- Weekly briefing home screen
- Basic settings

### What's OUT for V1 (add when validated):

### Insight Engine Data Model

The insight engine generates dollar projections by combining three inputs:

1. **Owner's operational data** (from CSV/Jobber): job history, pricing, volume, technician performance, customer records. Minimum viable data: 30+ jobs with dates, service types, and revenue amounts.
2. **Market benchmarks** (founder-curated for V1): The founder provides market rate ranges by trade and region based on consulting experience. Stored as structured data in Supabase. Benchmarks spot-checked against 2-3 public data sources (HomeAdvisor, Angi, BLS data) per trade before launch to establish baseline accuracy. V2: supplement with third-party data APIs or crowd-sourced pricing from the user base.
3. **Coaching knowledge base** (RAG): Trades-specific strategies for pricing, hiring, marketing, operations. Retrieved via pgvector semantic search.

**Dollar projection formula:** `(benchmark_rate - current_rate) x monthly_volume = projected_monthly_impact`. For non-pricing insights (e.g., customer retention), projections use: `(opportunity_count x avg_ticket x conversion_estimate)`. All projections labeled as estimates. Confidence indicator based on data volume: <50 jobs = "early estimate," 50-200 jobs = "based on your data," >200 jobs = "high confidence."

**Minimum data threshold:** The insight engine requires at least 30 jobs with dates and revenue to generate meaningful recommendations. Below that threshold, the product shows a "keep logging jobs — we need a bit more data to give you specific advice" message instead of guessing.

## Open Questions

1. **Will Josh actually act on push notifications?** The whole thesis rests on this. Need to validate before over-investing in the app experience.
2. **Email or SMS?** SMS has higher open rates but higher cost and regulatory requirements. Email is simpler to start but easier to ignore.
3. **Pricing model:** $200-400/month for coach replacement? Or freemium with premium insights? The coaching anchor suggests higher pricing, but Josh is price-sensitive (he underprices his own services).
4. **How much data is "enough"?** If Josh's Jobber data is sparse or messy, can the insight engine still generate useful recommendations? What's the minimum data threshold?
5. **Consultant angle as GTM:** Even with Approach A, the founder's consulting relationships are the distribution channel. Should the product have a "recommended by your consultant" onboarding flow?

## Success Criteria

- Josh (or 3 owners like him) opens at least 2 push notifications per week within the first month
- At least 1 owner takes a specific action based on a Cadence recommendation within 30 days
- Coach chat sessions average >3 messages (indicating real engagement, not tire-kicking)
- At least 1 owner willing to pay $200+/month after a 30-day trial
- Insight engine generates recommendations that reference specific data points (not generic advice) for >80% of outputs

## The Assignment

**Don't build yet.** Before writing a line of code:

1. Call Josh this week. Tell him: "I'm building a tool that would text you every Monday with one specific thing to do to make more money — based on your actual Jobber data. Like 'raise your backflow test price by $25, here's how.' Would you use that?" Listen to his reaction. Don't pitch — listen.

2. Do the same with 2 more clients. You need 3 real reactions, not 3 polite ones.

3. If at least 2 of 3 say something stronger than "that sounds cool" — something like "when can I get it?" or "how much?" or "I'd try that" — then build. If they shrug, dig into why before committing 4-6 weeks.

The spec is excellent. The thesis is strong. But a consultant building what they think clients need — without asking — is the #1 way software startups die in this space. 30 minutes of phone calls will save you months.
