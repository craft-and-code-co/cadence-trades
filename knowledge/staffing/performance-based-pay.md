# Performance-Based Pay — Commission Structures, Scorecards, and KPI Bonuses

Hourly-only pay creates a workforce that shows up, punches the clock, and goes home. Performance-based pay creates a team that thinks like owners. The goal isn't to replace base pay — it's to layer incentives on top that reward the behaviors and outcomes that grow your business.

## The Base + Commission Model

The most effective pay structure for service techs combines a competitive base hourly rate with commission on generated revenue.

**Recommended structure:**
- **Base pay:** Market rate for your area (typically $22-35/hr depending on trade, experience, and market)
- **Commission:** 6-10% on all repair and service revenue the tech generates
- **Cap consideration:** Some owners cap commission at 2x base pay to control costs. Others don't cap it — uncapped plans attract top performers

**Example:** A plumbing tech earning $28/hr base who generates $32,000/month in revenue at 8% commission:
- Base pay: $28 x 173 hours = $4,844
- Commission: $32,000 x 8% = $2,560
- **Total monthly: $7,404** ($42.80 effective hourly)

That tech is now earning top-of-market compensation, and you're paying it out of revenue they directly generated. Your cost on that commission is built into your pricing — it's not coming out of your profit.

## The Scorecard System

A tech scorecard tracks 4-6 KPIs monthly and ties a bonus to overall performance. This is more balanced than pure commission because it rewards quality, not just volume.

### Recommended Scorecard KPIs

| KPI | Target | Weight |
|-----|--------|--------|
| Revenue generated | $28,000+/month | 25% |
| Membership conversion rate | 25%+ of eligible calls | 20% |
| Option presentation rate | 90%+ (Better/Best offered) | 20% |
| Customer satisfaction score | 4.7+ out of 5 | 15% |
| Callback rate | Under 3% | 10% |
| Average ticket | $350+ (service, not including installs) | 10% |

### Bonus Calculation

Score each KPI as a percentage of target (capped at 120%), multiply by weight, sum for overall score.

| Overall Score | Monthly Bonus |
|--------------|---------------|
| 90-100% | $300 |
| 100-110% | $500 |
| 110-120% | $750 |
| 120%+ | $1,000 |

**Why scorecards work:** They prevent techs from gaming a single metric. A tech who hits high revenue but has a 6% callback rate won't score well. One who has great satisfaction scores but avoids presenting options won't either. The scorecard rewards well-rounded excellence.

## Install Crew Compensation

For install teams, the model is different because the work is project-based.

**Commission on installs:**
- **Lead installer:** 2-4% of installed job revenue
- **Helper/apprentice:** 1-2% of installed job revenue
- **Efficiency bonus:** Complete the install in fewer days than estimated = $100-250 bonus per day saved (with quality inspection required)

**Example:** A $12,000 HVAC changeout with lead installer at 3%:
- Commission: $360
- If completed in 1 day instead of 1.5: +$150 efficiency bonus
- **Total bonus on one job: $510**

A lead installer doing 3-4 installs per week can earn an extra $1,500-2,000/month in commissions and bonuses. That's significant retention power.

## KPI-Based Team Bonuses

Monthly or quarterly team bonuses create peer accountability and shared motivation.

**Structure:**
- Set a company-wide revenue or profit target for the month
- If the team hits the target, everyone gets a bonus
- Scale the bonus by role: techs get more, CSRs get less, but everyone participates

**Example quarterly bonus pool:**
| Company Revenue vs. Target | Bonus Pool |
|---------------------------|------------|
| 100-105% of target | $2,000 split across team |
| 105-110% of target | $4,000 split across team |
| 110%+ of target | $6,000 split across team |

This gets your dispatchers routing calls efficiently, your CSRs booking more calls, and your techs maximizing every opportunity — because everyone benefits when the company wins.

## Implementation Guidelines

**Start simple.** Don't launch commission, scorecards, and team bonuses all at once. Start with one element (usually commission or a membership SPIFF), run it for 90 days, then layer on the next.

**Communicate clearly.** Every tech should be able to calculate their expected bonus at any point in the month. If they can't, the system is too complex.

**Review quarterly.** Adjust targets based on seasonal demand. Your $28K monthly target in July (peak HVAC) might be $18K in October. Adjust so techs can realistically hit targets year-round.

**Never reduce base pay to fund incentives.** Performance pay is additive. If you cut base to add commission, you'll lose your team's trust and your best people.

**Make top earners visible.** Celebrate your highest-performing techs publicly. The recognition matters as much as the money, and it sets the standard for the rest of the team.
