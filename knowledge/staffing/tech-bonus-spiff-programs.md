# SPIFF and Bonus Programs That Actually Motivate Techs

A well-designed SPIFF (Sales Performance Incentive Fund) program turns your technicians into revenue generators without making them feel like used car salespeople. The key is aligning incentives with things that genuinely help the customer — memberships, necessary upgrades, and proper diagnostics — not pressuring them into selling things people don't need.

## Membership SPIFFs

This is the highest-impact SPIFF you can run. Every membership sold creates recurring revenue, improves customer retention, and fills your shoulder season schedule.

**Structure:**
- **$25-50 per membership sold** — paid on the next paycheck, not monthly
- **Bonus tier:** 10+ memberships in a month = $75 per membership (retroactive to all that month)
- **Renewal bonus:** $10-15 per renewal your customers complete (rewards relationship-building)

**Why it works:** A tech selling 8 memberships per month at $40/each earns an extra $320/month ($3,840/year). Meanwhile, those 8 memberships generate $1,200-2,400/month in recurring revenue for you. Everyone wins.

**Real example:** An HVAC company in Texas went from 200 to 1,400 active memberships in 18 months by paying techs $50/membership. The tech who sold the most earned an extra $14,000 that year. The company added $280,000 in annual recurring revenue.

## Upsell and Repair SPIFFs

Reward techs for presenting options and closing higher-tier repairs.

**Structure:**
- **3-5% commission on repair revenue above a daily target** — e.g., anything above $1,500/day in repair revenue earns 4% commission
- **"Better/Best" bonus:** $15-25 every time a customer selects the Better or Best option (encourages proper option presentation)
- **Equipment lead bonus:** $50-100 for every qualified equipment replacement lead that converts to a sold job

**Setting daily targets:** Look at your current average revenue per tech per day. If it's $1,200, set the SPIFF threshold at $1,400. You want it achievable but requiring effort.

**Why it works:** Techs start thinking about presenting complete solutions rather than minimum fixes. A tech who consistently earns $200-400/month in upsell SPIFFs is generating $4,000-8,000/month in additional revenue you wouldn't have captured otherwise.

## Revenue Tier Bonuses

Monthly bonuses based on total revenue generated, paid in addition to per-item SPIFFs.

**Structure:**
| Monthly Revenue Generated | Bonus |
|--------------------------|-------|
| $20,000 - $25,000 | $200 |
| $25,001 - $30,000 | $400 |
| $30,001 - $35,000 | $650 |
| $35,001+ | $1,000 |

**Why it works:** Creates a cumulative motivation throughout the month. A tech at $24,500 on the 28th will hustle to cross the $25K threshold. The bonus cost is 1.3-2.8% of the revenue generated — well within your margins.

## Customer Satisfaction SPIFFs

Tie a portion of bonuses to quality, not just revenue.

**Structure:**
- **$25 bonus per 5-star Google review** that mentions the tech by name
- **Monthly quality bonus:** $100-200 for zero callbacks in a calendar month
- **"Perfect call" bonus:** $15 per call with a post-visit survey score of 9 or 10 out of 10

**Why it works:** Prevents the "sell at all costs" mentality. Techs know that a callback or bad review costs them money, so they focus on doing the job right and communicating honestly.

## Implementation Rules

**Pay SPIFFs quickly.** Weekly or on the next paycheck. Monthly payouts lose their motivational power. The closer the reward is to the behavior, the stronger the reinforcement.

**Make it visible.** Post a leaderboard in the shop (digital or whiteboard). Techs are competitive. Seeing their name at the top — or not — is a powerful motivator.

**Keep it simple.** If your techs need a spreadsheet to figure out what they earned, it's too complicated. They should be able to calculate their bonus in their head after each call.

**Track everything.** Use your service software to automatically track memberships sold, revenue per tech, option selection rates, and callbacks. Don't rely on self-reporting.

**Set guardrails.** SPIFFs should never incentivize unnecessary work. Make it clear: selling a customer something they don't need is a fireable offense, not a bonus opportunity. Audit repair recommendations monthly.

## Budget Guidelines

Total SPIFF/bonus payout should be 3-6% of the tech's generated revenue. If a tech generates $30,000/month in revenue and earns $1,200 in bonuses, that's 4% — right in the sweet spot. You're paying $1,200 to generate revenue that wouldn't exist without the incentive. That's not a cost — it's an investment with a 10-25x return.
