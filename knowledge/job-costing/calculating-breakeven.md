# Calculating Your Breakeven Point — Fixed Costs, Contribution Margin, and the Breakeven Formula

Your breakeven point is the minimum revenue your business needs to generate just to cover all costs — not to profit, not to grow, just to keep the lights on and make payroll. Every dollar above breakeven is profit. Every dollar below is a loss. Knowing this number changes how you make decisions about pricing, hiring, marketing, and overhead.

## The Breakeven Formula

**Breakeven Revenue = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin Percentage**

That's it. Two inputs. Let's break them down.

## Step 1: Calculate Your Fixed Costs

Fixed costs are the expenses that don't change based on how many jobs you run. You pay them whether you run 200 calls this month or zero.

**Monthly fixed costs for a typical 5-tech trades company:**

| Fixed Cost | Monthly Amount |
|-----------|---------------|
| Owner's salary | $8,500 |
| Office staff (2 CSRs + office manager) | $11,000 |
| Office rent | $2,200 |
| Vehicle payments (5 trucks) | $4,500 |
| Vehicle insurance | $1,800 |
| General liability + umbrella insurance | $1,500 |
| Workers' comp insurance | $2,800 |
| Software (service, CRM, accounting) | $1,600 |
| Marketing budget | $4,000 |
| Phone/internet | $600 |
| Accounting/legal | $1,200 |
| Loan payments | $1,500 |
| Misc (supplies, office, etc.) | $800 |
| **Total Monthly Fixed Costs** | **$42,000** |

Your fixed costs are $42,000/month or $504,000/year. That's the nut you have to crack every single month before you make a penny of profit.

## Step 2: Calculate Your Contribution Margin

The contribution margin is what's left from each revenue dollar after paying the variable (direct) costs for that specific job. Variable costs are the costs that scale with each job — tech labor, materials, and any job-specific expenses.

**Formula:** Contribution Margin % = (Revenue - Variable Costs) / Revenue

**Example:**
- Average service call revenue: $450
- Average variable costs per call:
  - Tech labor (burdened): $72 (1.5 hrs x $48/hr)
  - Materials: $55
  - Total variable: $127
- Contribution margin per call: $450 - $127 = $323
- **Contribution margin percentage: $323 / $450 = 71.8%**

This means every dollar of revenue contributes $0.718 toward covering your fixed costs and generating profit.

## Step 3: Calculate Breakeven

**Breakeven Revenue = $42,000 / 0.718 = $58,496/month**

Your company needs to generate $58,496/month in service revenue just to break even. That's roughly:
- $58,496 / $450 average ticket = **130 service calls per month**
- 130 / 22 working days = **5.9 calls per day** across your team
- With 5 techs, that's **1.2 calls per tech per day** just to break even

That's reassuring — most techs should be running 4-6 calls per day. But it also means if you lose a tech and can't replace them quickly, or if a slow week hits, you feel it immediately.

## Using Breakeven for Decisions

### Should I Hire Another Tech?

Adding a tech increases your fixed costs (vehicle, insurance, phone, tools) by roughly $3,000-4,000/month. New breakeven:

$42,000 + $3,500 = $45,500 monthly fixed costs
$45,500 / 0.718 = **$63,370/month breakeven**

That tech needs to generate at least $4,874/month in revenue ($63,370 - $58,496) just to cover their additional overhead. At $450/average ticket, that's 11 calls per month — less than one call per working day. If they can run 3+ calls per day, the hire is a no-brainer.

### Should I Increase My Marketing Budget?

If you increase marketing by $2,000/month, your new breakeven is:
$44,000 / 0.718 = **$61,281/month**

That $2,000 needs to generate at least $2,785 in additional monthly revenue ($61,281 - $58,496) to justify itself. That's about 6 additional service calls per month. If $2,000 in marketing generates 6+ calls, it's profitable.

### Should I Open a Second Location?

A second location might add $15,000-25,000/month in fixed costs (rent, manager, additional vehicles). Your breakeven jumps significantly. Run the numbers before signing a lease. The new location needs to generate enough revenue to cover its own breakeven independently.

## Breakeven by Revenue Stream

If you have multiple revenue streams (service, installation, maintenance), calculate breakeven for each:

| Stream | Revenue | Variable Cost % | Contribution Margin % |
|--------|---------|----------------|----------------------|
| Service/Repair | $75,000 | 28% | 72% |
| Installation | $50,000 | 58% | 42% |
| Maintenance | $15,000 | 38% | 62% |
| **Blended** | **$140,000** | **38.6%** | **61.4%** |

Blended breakeven: $42,000 / 0.614 = **$68,404/month**

Notice that installation has a much lower contribution margin. If you shift your revenue mix toward more service work and less installation, your blended contribution margin improves and your breakeven drops. This is why service-focused companies are often more profitable than install-heavy companies at the same revenue level.

## The Daily Breakeven Number

Turn your monthly breakeven into a daily target that everyone can understand:

**Daily breakeven = Monthly breakeven / Working days per month**

$58,496 / 22 = **$2,659/day**

Post this number in your dispatch area. Every day your team generates more than $2,659, you're making profit. Below that, you're losing money. This simple metric creates urgency and accountability.

**Per-tech daily breakeven:** $2,659 / 5 techs = **$532/day per tech**

If a tech isn't generating at least $532/day in revenue, they're not covering their share of the overhead. This isn't about pressure — it's about awareness. Techs who understand the breakeven number make different decisions in the field about presenting options, selling memberships, and managing their time.

## Review Quarterly

Your breakeven changes as your fixed costs change. Recalculate every quarter when you review financials. If breakeven is creeping up faster than revenue, you have an overhead problem that needs attention before it becomes a cash flow crisis.
