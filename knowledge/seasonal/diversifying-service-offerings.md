# Diversifying Your Service Offerings — Adding Revenue Streams That Smooth Out Seasonality

The most dangerous number in business is one — one trade, one season, one revenue stream. If 80% of your revenue comes from AC repair in summer and furnace repair in winter, those shoulder months in spring and fall will bleed you dry. Diversification isn't about becoming a jack-of-all-trades. It's about strategically adding services that use your existing skills, trucks, and team to generate revenue year-round.

## Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) — For HVAC Companies

IAQ is the highest-margin add-on for HVAC companies and sells year-round. Post-COVID, homeowners care about air quality more than ever.

**Products to offer:**
- **UV lights (germicidal):** $800-1,500 installed. Cost: $150-300 in parts, 1 hour of labor. Margin: 65-75%.
- **Air purifiers (whole-home):** $1,200-2,500 installed. Brands like REME HALO, iWave, Aprilaire. Margin: 55-65%.
- **Humidifiers/dehumidifiers:** $500-1,800 installed. Essential in climates with extreme humidity swings.
- **Air duct cleaning:** $299-599 per home. Subcontract if you don't own equipment, or buy a system ($8,000-15,000) if demand justifies it.
- **High-efficiency filters:** MERV 13+ filters, subscription or one-time sales

**How to sell IAQ:** During every maintenance visit and repair call, have your techs present an IAQ recommendation. "Based on what I'm seeing in your ductwork, I'd recommend a UV light to kill mold and bacteria. It installs right in your air handler and runs 24/7."

**Revenue potential:** If 15% of your maintenance and repair calls result in an IAQ add-on averaging $1,200, and you run 200 calls/month, that's 30 IAQ sales = $36,000/month in additional revenue at 60%+ margins.

## Water Treatment — For Plumbing Companies

Every home has water issues — hardness, chlorine taste, contaminants. Water treatment is a natural extension of plumbing that provides high-margin installation revenue plus recurring filter replacement income.

**Products to offer:**
- **Water softeners:** $1,500-3,500 installed. Essential in hard water areas.
- **Reverse osmosis systems (under-sink):** $500-1,200 installed. Filters and maintenance sold annually.
- **Whole-home water filtration:** $1,200-3,000 installed.
- **Filter replacement programs:** Recurring revenue — $150-300/year per customer for filter changes.

**How to get started:** Partner with a water treatment manufacturer (Kinetico, WaterBoss, Pentair) who provides training and marketing support. Most will train your techs in 1-2 days.

**Revenue potential:** Water treatment adds $8,000-15,000/month for a typical residential plumbing company within the first year.

## Generators — For Electrical Companies

Standby generator installation is a $5,000-15,000+ job with strong margins, and demand is growing as homeowners experience more frequent power outages.

**Revenue model:**
- **Standby generator installation:** $5,000-15,000 (Generac, Kohler, Briggs & Stratton). Your cost: $3,000-8,000 in equipment + $500-1,000 in labor.
- **Maintenance agreements:** $200-400/year per generator. Annual load testing, oil change, filter replacement.
- **Portable generator hookup panels:** $800-2,000 installed. Quick job, good margin.

**Seasonality benefit:** Generator sales spike after major storms but can be marketed year-round with "peace of mind" messaging. Maintenance visits fill slow months.

**How to get started:** Become an authorized dealer for Generac or Kohler. They provide training, marketing materials, and lead generation through their dealer locator websites.

## EV Charger Installation — For Electrical Companies

EV adoption is accelerating, and every new EV owner needs a Level 2 charger at home. This is a growing, high-margin service line.

**Revenue model:**
- **Level 2 charger installation:** $1,200-3,000 (including charger). Your cost: $400-800 in parts + 2-4 hours labor.
- **Panel upgrade (often required):** $1,500-3,500 additional if the panel can't handle the load.

**Revenue potential:** Even in moderate EV adoption markets, this adds $5,000-10,000/month in installation revenue.

## Adding a Second Trade

If you're a single-trade company, adding a complementary trade is the biggest diversification move you can make.

**Natural trade combinations:**
- HVAC + Plumbing (most common — shared customer base, complementary seasons)
- Electrical + HVAC (electrical work is required for most HVAC installs)
- Plumbing + Backflow (same skill set, different customer segments)
- HVAC + Electrical + Plumbing (the "full-service home" model)

### How to Add a Trade Responsibly

**Step 1: Start with one tech.** Hire an experienced licensed tech in the new trade. Don't try to cross-train your existing team initially.

**Step 2: Serve your existing customers first.** Market the new trade to your current customer base. You already have their trust. "Now offering plumbing services — same great team, same commitment to quality."

**Step 3: Start with high-margin services.** Don't try to do everything in the new trade right away. Start with the top 5-10 services that have the best margin and the most demand from your existing customers.

**Step 4: Build the team.** Once demand justifies it (consistent 2-3 calls/day in the new trade), hire a second tech and begin building the department.

**Step 5: Cross-sell relentlessly.** Every HVAC call is a chance to mention plumbing services and vice versa. Bundle maintenance plans across trades. Create whole-home protection packages.

### The Revenue Impact

Companies that add a second trade typically see 30-50% revenue growth within 18 months. More importantly, they smooth out seasonal dips because each trade has different peak and slow periods. When HVAC is dead in October, plumbing is picking up for winter.

## The Decision Framework

Before adding any service, ask:
1. **Do my existing customers want this?** (Ask them — survey or conversation)
2. **Can my current team deliver it?** (Or do I need to hire?)
3. **Does it use my existing infrastructure?** (Trucks, tools, office, brand)
4. **What's the margin?** (Target 50%+ gross margin on new services)
5. **Does it fill a slow season?** (Prioritize services that generate revenue when you need it most)

If the answer is yes to 4 out of 5, it's worth pursuing. Start small, prove the demand, then scale.
