# Pre-Season Demand Generation — Early Bird Campaigns, Tune-Up Pushes, and Email Sequences

The best trades companies don't wait for the phone to ring when the heat wave hits or the first freeze arrives. They generate demand 6-8 weeks before peak season, filling their calendar with booked work before their competitors even start advertising. Pre-season campaigns are the highest-ROI marketing you'll ever do because you're reaching customers when competition for their attention is lowest.

## Early Bird Campaigns

Early bird pricing creates urgency and rewards customers for booking during your slow period instead of waiting until peak demand.

### Structure

**The offer:** A meaningful discount (15-25%) or added value on seasonal services booked before a specific date.

**HVAC example:**
- "Book your AC tune-up by April 15th — $89 (regular price $129). Includes FREE filter and priority summer scheduling."
- Available to all existing customers and membership holders
- After April 15, price returns to $129 for non-members

**Plumbing example:**
- "Schedule your water heater flush before October 31st — $79 (regular $119). We'll include a free anode rod inspection ($49 value)."

**Why this works:** You're filling March and April (dead months for HVAC) with pre-booked tune-ups instead of scrambling in June. Customers feel like they got a deal. Your techs stay busy. And during those tune-ups, you'll find repair opportunities — a cracked heat exchanger, a failing capacitor, aging equipment. Those tune-up calls generate 30-40% of your shoulder-season repair revenue.

## The Email Sequence

Email is the most cost-effective channel for pre-season campaigns because you're reaching people who already know you. Your customer email list is a gold mine — use it.

### 4-Email Pre-Season Sequence

**Email 1 — Education (6 weeks before peak)**
Subject: "Is your AC ready for summer? 5 signs it's not"
Content: Educational content about warning signs of system problems. No hard sell. Establish expertise and plant the seed that maintenance matters.
CTA: "Schedule your tune-up" (soft)

**Email 2 — Early Bird Offer (5 weeks before peak)**
Subject: "Early bird AC tune-up — $89 through April 15"
Content: The offer, what's included, and why booking early matters (shorter wait times, catch problems before they become emergencies).
CTA: "Book now and save $40" with a direct scheduling link or phone number

**Email 3 — Social Proof (3 weeks before peak)**
Subject: "Here's why 400+ homeowners trust us for their spring tune-up"
Content: 2-3 customer testimonials, your Google review rating, and a reminder of the early bird deadline.
CTA: "Join them — book your tune-up before [date]"

**Email 4 — Urgency / Last Chance (1 week before deadline)**
Subject: "Last chance — early bird pricing ends Friday"
Content: Final reminder with countdown urgency. Mention that summer appointments fill up fast and early bookers get priority scheduling.
CTA: "Book now — early bird pricing ends [date]"

### Email Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 25-35% |
| Click rate | 3-6% |
| Booking conversion | 2-4% of list |

If your email list has 3,000 customers and 3% book from the sequence, that's 90 tune-ups booked — $7,000-11,000 in direct revenue plus the repair opportunities those visits generate.

## Tune-Up Push Tactics

### Direct Mail

For customers over 55 (who respond better to physical mail), send a postcard 4-6 weeks before peak season.

- **Format:** Oversized postcard (6x9 or larger)
- **Front:** Compelling offer with a deadline
- **Back:** 3 benefits, 2-3 testimonials, clear phone number and QR code for online booking
- **Cost:** $0.50-0.75 per piece (printing + postage)
- **Expected response rate:** 1-3%

Send to your customer list first (2-3% response rate). Then to a targeted mailing list of homeowners in your service area (0.5-1.5% response rate).

### Text Message Campaigns

Text messages have a 98% open rate vs. 25-35% for email. Use them strategically.

**Pre-season text (one message):**
> "Hi [Name], it's [Company]. Spring is coming — let's make sure your AC is ready. Book your tune-up by April 15 for just $89 (reg. $129). Reply BOOK or call [phone]. Reply STOP to opt out."

**Rules for text marketing:**
- Only text customers who have opted in (TCPA compliance is serious)
- Maximum 2-3 texts per campaign (don't spam)
- Always include opt-out language
- Send between 9am-6pm local time

### Social Media Push

Run Facebook and Instagram ads (see Facebook ads guide) starting 6 weeks before peak season with your early bird offer. Retarget your website visitors and email list with the specific offer.

## Measuring Pre-Season Campaign Success

Track these metrics for every pre-season campaign:

- **Tune-ups booked** during the campaign period
- **Revenue from tune-up visits** (including repair revenue discovered during tune-ups)
- **Memberships sold** during tune-up visits
- **Cost per booked tune-up** (marketing spend / tune-ups booked)
- **Equipment replacement leads** generated from tune-up findings

A successful pre-season campaign pays for itself 5-10x over when you account for the repair revenue, memberships sold, and equipment leads generated from those tune-up calls.
