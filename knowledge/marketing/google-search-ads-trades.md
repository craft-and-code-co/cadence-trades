# Google Search Ads — Keyword Strategy, Ad Copy, and Landing Pages for Trades

Google Search ads are the highest-intent marketing channel for trades companies. When someone types "AC repair near me" at 2pm in July, they're not browsing — they're buying. That's a customer ready to book right now, and your ad needs to be there. Here's how to set it up properly.

## Keyword Strategy

### High-Intent Keywords (Priority 1 — 50% of budget)

These are people with an immediate need. They convert at the highest rate.

**HVAC:**
- "ac repair near me," "ac not working," "emergency hvac repair"
- "furnace repair [city]," "heater not working," "no heat"
- "ac replacement [city]," "new ac unit cost"

**Plumbing:**
- "plumber near me," "emergency plumber [city]"
- "water heater repair," "leaking pipe repair"
- "drain cleaning [city]," "clogged drain service"

**Electrical:**
- "electrician near me," "emergency electrician [city]"
- "panel upgrade [city]," "outlet not working"
- "generator installation [city]"

**Backflow:**
- "backflow testing [city]," "backflow certification near me"
- "backflow preventer repair," "annual backflow test"

### Service-Specific Keywords (Priority 2 — 30% of budget)

Target specific services that are higher-ticket and less competitive.

- "tankless water heater installation [city]"
- "ductless mini split installation"
- "whole house rewire cost"
- "sewer line replacement [city]"
- "EV charger installation"

### Brand Defense Keywords (Priority 3 — 10% of budget)

Bid on your own company name. Competitors will bid on your name if you don't, and you'll lose clicks to them.

### Competitor Keywords (Priority 4 — 10% of budget)

Bid on competitor names in your area. Cost per click is higher, but you're stealing customers who are actively looking for a service provider. Don't use competitor names in your ad copy — that's against Google's policies. Just bid on the keywords.

## Negative Keywords (Critical)

Add these on day one to stop wasting money:
- "DIY," "how to," "YouTube," "tutorial" (information seekers, not buyers)
- "jobs," "hiring," "careers," "salary" (job seekers)
- "free," "cheap," "cheapest" (bargain hunters)
- "training," "school," "certification" (students)
- "reviews," "complaints," "BBB" (researchers, not buyers)

Review your search terms report weekly for the first month and add new negatives as you spot irrelevant queries.

## Ad Copy That Converts

### Headlines (max 30 characters each — you get up to 15)
- "[City] AC Repair — Same Day"
- "Licensed & Insured Plumbers"
- "24/7 Emergency Service"
- "Upfront Pricing — No Surprises"
- "5-Star Rated — 500+ Reviews"
- "$50 Off Your First Service"
- "Free Estimates on Replacements"

### Descriptions (max 90 characters each — you get up to 4)
- "Flat rate pricing so you know the cost before we start. Licensed, insured, background-checked techs. Book online or call now."
- "Same-day service available. We show up on time, diagnose the problem, and give you options — not pressure. Call [phone]."

### Ad Extensions (Use all of these)
- **Call extension:** Your tracking phone number
- **Location extension:** Your Google Business Profile
- **Sitelink extensions:** "AC Repair," "Water Heater Service," "Maintenance Plans," "Free Estimate"
- **Callout extensions:** "Same-Day Service," "Upfront Pricing," "Licensed & Insured," "Satisfaction Guaranteed"
- **Structured snippets:** Types: "AC Repair, Furnace Repair, Duct Cleaning, Maintenance Plans"

## Landing Pages

**Never send ad traffic to your homepage.** Your homepage has navigation, blog links, and a dozen distractions. Your landing page has one job: get the visitor to call or fill out a form.

### Landing Page Must-Haves

1. **Headline matching the ad** — If the ad says "AC Repair in [City]," the landing page headline should say the same thing
2. **Phone number — large, clickable, above the fold** — 60-70% of trades leads come from phone calls, not forms
3. **Short form** — Name, phone, service needed, preferred time. That's it. Every extra field reduces conversions.
4. **Social proof** — Google review stars, review count, 2-3 short testimonials
5. **Trust signals** — License number, insurance, "background-checked technicians," BBB logo, manufacturer badges
6. **Offer** — Match whatever the ad promised ($50 off, free estimate, etc.)
7. **No navigation menu** — Remove the header nav. The only actions should be call or submit the form.

### Conversion Rate Benchmarks
- Good: 8-12% of landing page visitors convert (call or form)
- Great: 12-18%
- Below 5%: Your landing page needs work

## Budget and Bidding

**Starting budget:** $1,500-3,000/month for a single trade in one market. $3,000-6,000 if you serve multiple trades.

**Bidding strategy:** Start with Maximize Conversions with a target CPA. Set your target CPA at your acceptable cost per lead ($30-75 for most trades).

**Cost benchmarks:**
- Cost per click: $8-25 (trades are competitive)
- Cost per lead: $30-75
- Cost per booked job: $80-200
- Target ROAS: 5-8x (spend $2,000, generate $10,000-16,000)

**When to increase budget:** When you're consistently hitting your target CPA and have capacity for more calls. Increase by 20% increments and monitor for 2 weeks before increasing again.
