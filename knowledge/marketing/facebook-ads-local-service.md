# Facebook Ads for Local Trades and Home Service Companies

Facebook ads work for trades companies — but not the way most owners think. You're not selling HVAC repairs on Facebook like someone sells shoes online. Nobody wakes up and scrolls Facebook looking for a plumber. Instead, Facebook is a top-of-funnel awareness and offer-driven platform. You're putting your name in front of homeowners in your service area so that when they need you, you're the first company they think of.

## Campaign Structure

Run three types of campaigns simultaneously for best results:

### 1. Seasonal Offer Campaigns (60% of budget)

These are your money-makers. Tie them to seasonal demand with a clear, time-limited offer.

**HVAC examples:**
- "AC Tune-Up — $89 (reg. $149) — Book by May 31st"
- "Furnace Safety Inspection — $79 — Limited Spots Available"
- "Free Second Opinion on Any Repair Over $500"

**Plumbing examples:**
- "Water Heater Flush — $99 — Prevent Costly Breakdowns"
- "Whole-Home Plumbing Inspection — $49 (reg. $149)"
- "Tankless Water Heater Install — $500 Off This Month"

**Electrical examples:**
- "Whole-Home Surge Protection — $299 Installed"
- "Generator Install — Free Load Assessment ($250 Value)"
- "LED Recessed Lighting — 6 Lights Installed for $999"

**Creative format:** Video works best (2-3x the engagement of static images). A 30-60 second video of your tech explaining the offer, shot on an iPhone, outperforms polished studio content. Show real people, real trucks, real homes.

### 2. Brand Awareness Campaigns (25% of budget)

Keep your company top of mind year-round with educational and trust-building content.

- Customer testimonial videos (ask happy customers for a 30-second video review)
- Before/after project photos with captions
- "Did you know?" educational posts (e.g., "Your AC filter should be changed every 60-90 days")
- Team spotlights and behind-the-scenes content

These don't drive immediate calls, but they build familiarity. When a homeowner's AC dies in July, they'll remember the company they've been seeing for months.

### 3. Retargeting Campaigns (15% of budget)

Show ads to people who visited your website but didn't book. This requires the Meta pixel installed on your site.

- Retarget website visitors from the last 30 days
- Show a stronger offer or social proof (reviews, awards)
- "Still need help? Book today and save $50"

Retargeting audiences convert at 3-5x the rate of cold audiences. This is the easiest money in your ad budget.

## Targeting Settings

**Location:** Your service area only. Use a radius around your shop or drop pins on your service zip codes. Exclude areas you don't service — wasted impressions are wasted dollars.

**Age:** 28-65+. Homeowners skew older. Exclude under 25 unless you're in a college town with rental properties.

**Interests:** Layer these for better targeting:
- Homeownership, home improvement, DIY (to find homeowners)
- Your specific trade keywords (HVAC, plumbing, electrical)
- Home warranty, home insurance (indicates homeowner mindset)

**Income targeting:** If available in your area, target household income in the top 50%. Higher-income homeowners spend more per job and are more likely to choose premium options.

**Exclusions:** Exclude renters, apartments, and people who already follow your page (for acquisition campaigns).

## Budget Guidelines

**Starting budget:** $500-1,500/month. This is enough to reach 15,000-40,000 homeowners in a typical service area multiple times per month.

**Scaling:** Once you're seeing a consistent cost per lead under $50, increase budget by 25% per month until cost per lead starts rising.

**Cost benchmarks for trades:**
- Cost per click: $2-8
- Cost per lead (form fill or call): $25-75
- Cost per booked job: $75-200
- Target return: 5-10x ad spend (spend $1,000, generate $5,000-10,000 in revenue)

## Tracking What Works

**Call tracking number.** Use a dedicated phone number on your Facebook ads (via CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics, or similar). This lets you count exactly how many calls came from Facebook.

**Lead forms vs. landing pages.** Facebook's native lead forms (instant forms) get more submissions because the user never leaves Facebook. But the lead quality is often lower. Test both — some markets do better with landing pages that require more effort.

**Track to the dollar.** Don't just count leads. Track which leads booked, what revenue they generated, and what your cost per acquired customer was. If your average job is $400 and your cost to acquire that customer was $75, your return is 5.3x. That's a great ad campaign.

## The Biggest Mistake

Running one ad with $200/month and calling it quits after 3 weeks because "Facebook doesn't work." Facebook ads are a volume and consistency game. You need enough budget to reach your audience frequently, enough time to optimize (30-60 days minimum), and enough creative variety to keep people engaged. Commit to 90 days before you evaluate.
