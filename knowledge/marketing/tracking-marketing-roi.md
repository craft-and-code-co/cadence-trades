# Tracking Marketing ROI — Call Tracking, Cost Per Lead, and Revenue Attribution

Most trades companies spend $3,000-10,000/month on marketing and have no idea which half is working. They know calls come in, but they can't tell you whether those calls came from Google ads, Facebook, the truck wrap, or a yard sign. If you can't track it, you can't improve it. Here's how to measure your marketing down to the dollar.

## Call Tracking: The Foundation

70-80% of leads for trades companies come via phone calls. If you're not tracking which calls come from which marketing source, you're flying blind.

### How Call Tracking Works

You assign a unique phone number to each marketing channel. All numbers forward to your main line — the customer experience doesn't change. But now you know exactly where each call originated.

**Typical tracking number setup:**
| Channel | Tracking Number | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| Google Search Ads | (555) 100-2001 | Dynamic number insertion on landing page |
| Google LSA | Built-in | LSA tracks leads natively |
| Facebook Ads | (555) 100-2002 | Static number on ad creative |
| Website (organic) | (555) 100-2003 | Dynamic, shows based on traffic source |
| Google Business Profile | (555) 100-2004 | Listed as primary number on GBP |
| Direct mail | (555) 100-2005 | Printed on mailers |
| Yard signs | (555) 100-2006 | Printed on signs |

**Recommended tools:** CallRail ($45-145/month), CallTrackingMetrics ($39-99/month), or WhatConverts ($30-100/month). These tools record calls, track sources, and integrate with your CRM.

### What to Track Per Call

- Source (which marketing channel)
- Call duration (calls under 60 seconds are usually not real leads)
- Whether it was answered or missed
- Whether it booked (mark in your CRM)
- Revenue from the booked job

## The Metrics That Matter

### Cost Per Lead (CPL)

**Formula:** Total marketing spend on channel / Number of leads from that channel

**Example:** You spent $2,000 on Google Search ads last month and received 42 calls over 60 seconds. CPL = $2,000 / 42 = **$47.62 per lead**

**Benchmarks by channel:**
| Channel | Typical CPL |
|---------|------------|
| Google LSA | $15-45 |
| Google Search Ads | $30-75 |
| Facebook Ads | $25-75 |
| Direct Mail | $50-150 |
| SEO (organic) | $10-30 (ongoing cost of SEO / leads) |
| Referrals | $0-15 (referral bonus cost) |

### Cost Per Booked Job

**Formula:** Total marketing spend on channel / Number of jobs booked from that channel

Not every lead books. Your booking rate (lead-to-job conversion) typically runs 35-55% depending on your CSR team and the channel.

**Example:** 42 leads from Google ads, 18 booked (43% booking rate). Cost per booked job = $2,000 / 18 = **$111.11**

### Revenue Per Marketing Dollar (ROAS)

**Formula:** Total revenue from channel / Total spend on channel

**Example:** Those 18 booked jobs generated $14,400 in revenue. ROAS = $14,400 / $2,000 = **7.2x**

That means every $1 you spent on Google ads returned $7.20 in revenue. Anything above 5x is good. Above 8x is excellent.

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Formula:** Total marketing + sales costs / Number of new customers acquired

This is the big-picture number. If you spend $6,000/month on all marketing and acquire 45 new customers, your CAC is $133.33.

Compare CAC to your average customer lifetime value (LTV). If a customer spends $800/year on maintenance and repairs and stays with you for 5 years, LTV = $4,000. A $133 CAC to acquire a $4,000 customer is a 30:1 return.

## Revenue Attribution: Closing the Loop

The real power comes when you connect marketing source to actual revenue — not just leads.

### How to Set This Up

1. **Call tracking** identifies the source of every inbound call
2. **Your CRM or service software** (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber) records the job and revenue
3. **Connect them:** Most call tracking tools integrate directly with service software. When a call comes in via tracking number, it tags the customer record with the marketing source.
4. **Monthly reporting:** Pull a report showing revenue by marketing source

### Monthly Marketing Scorecard

Build this report every month:

| Channel | Spend | Leads | CPL | Booked Jobs | Revenue | ROAS |
|---------|-------|-------|-----|-------------|---------|------|
| Google LSA | $800 | 28 | $28.57 | 15 | $12,750 | 15.9x |
| Google Search | $2,000 | 42 | $47.62 | 18 | $14,400 | 7.2x |
| Facebook | $1,200 | 22 | $54.55 | 9 | $6,300 | 5.3x |
| SEO/Organic | $500 | 35 | $14.29 | 16 | $11,200 | 22.4x |
| Direct Mail | $1,500 | 12 | $125.00 | 5 | $4,750 | 3.2x |
| **Total** | **$6,000** | **139** | **$43.17** | **63** | **$49,400** | **8.2x** |

This scorecard tells you immediately: LSA and organic are your best performers. Direct mail is underperforming — either fix it or reallocate that budget. Google Search and Facebook are solid middle performers worth maintaining.

## Taking Action on the Data

**Double down on winners.** If LSAs are generating leads at $28 each, max out your LSA budget before adding a dollar anywhere else.

**Fix or kill losers.** If a channel is below 3x ROAS after 90 days of optimization, cut it and reallocate.

**Test constantly.** Run A/B tests on ad copy, landing pages, and offers. A small improvement in conversion rate (10% to 13%) can drop your CPL by 20%.

**Review monthly, decide quarterly.** Look at the scorecard every month, but make budget allocation decisions on a quarterly basis. Monthly fluctuations are normal — trends over 90 days are what matter.
