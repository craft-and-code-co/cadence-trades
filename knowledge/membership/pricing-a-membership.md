# How to Price Your Maintenance Membership

Pricing a membership wrong is the fastest way to kill your program. Price too low and you'll lose money on every visit, resent the members, and your techs will treat membership calls as low-priority. Price too high and nobody signs up. The sweet spot is a price where you break even (or take a small loss) on the maintenance visits themselves, but profit handsomely from the repair revenue, retention, and referrals that members generate.

## The Cost-Based Pricing Method

Start with what it actually costs you to deliver the membership.

### Step 1: Calculate Your Cost Per Visit

**Labor:** Average time on a maintenance call x burdened labor rate
- HVAC tune-up: 1.0-1.5 hours x $48/hr = $48-72
- Plumbing inspection: 1.0-1.5 hours x $48/hr = $48-72
- Electrical inspection: 1.0-1.25 hours x $48/hr = $48-60

**Materials consumed per visit:**
- HVAC: Filter ($5-15), chemicals/cleaners ($3-5), misc supplies ($5) = $13-25
- Plumbing: Water heater flush supplies ($5-10), test kits ($3) = $8-13
- Electrical: Testing supplies, batteries for detectors ($5-10) = $5-10

**Truck/overhead per visit:** $25-40 (fuel, vehicle wear, dispatch, CRM costs)

**Total cost per HVAC tune-up visit:** $86-137

For a 2-visit HVAC plan: $172-274 in total annual delivery cost.

### Step 2: Set Your Membership Price

**The break-even approach:** Price the membership at or slightly above your delivery cost. Your profit comes from elsewhere (see below).

**Typical annual pricing by trade:**

| Plan Type | Annual Price | Monthly Price |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| HVAC - 1 visit | $99-149 | $9.99-14.99 |
| HVAC - 2 visits | $179-249 | $16.99-22.99 |
| Plumbing - 1 visit | $99-149 | $9.99-14.99 |
| Electrical - 1 visit | $89-129 | $8.99-12.99 |
| Backflow - annual test | $79-119 | $7.99-10.99 |
| Whole-home bundle (all trades) | $349-499 | $32.99-44.99 |

### Step 3: Validate with Perceived Value

Calculate the retail value of everything included and make sure your membership price is 40-60% of that value. This is what makes the membership feel like a deal.

**HVAC Premium Plan example:**
| Included Item | Retail Value |
|--------------|-------------|
| Spring AC tune-up | $149 |
| Fall furnace tune-up | $149 |
| 2 standard filters | $30 |
| Priority scheduling | $50 value |
| No overtime charges | $100 value |
| 20% repair discount | Variable |
| **Total perceived value** | **$478+** |
| **Membership price** | **$219/year** |

The customer sees: "I'm getting $478 worth of service for $219." That's a 54% savings — compelling enough to sign up.

## Annual vs. Monthly Pricing

Offer both, but structure them so monthly costs more.

**Annual:** $219/year (paid upfront)
**Monthly:** $19.99/month ($239.88/year)

The annual option improves your cash flow and locks the customer in. The monthly option removes the barrier of a larger upfront payment. About 60% of members will choose monthly, 40% annual. That's fine — monthly members have higher lifetime value because they rarely cancel (inertia is powerful).

### Auto-Pay is Non-Negotiable

All memberships should be on automatic payment — credit card or ACH. Do not invoice memberships manually. The administrative cost of chasing payments will eat your margin, and members who have to actively pay each month are far more likely to cancel.

## Where the Real Money Is

The membership visit itself might break even or even lose $20-30. That's by design. Here's where you profit:

**Repair revenue from members.** Members call you for every repair because they already trust you and get a discount. A member household spends 2.5-3.5x more annually than a non-member. If your average non-member customer spends $400/year, a member spends $1,000-1,400/year.

**Retention.** Non-member customer retention is 20-30% year over year. Member retention is 70-85%. You stop bleeding customers to competitors.

**Reduced marketing cost.** Every member you retain is a customer you don't need to re-acquire. If your customer acquisition cost is $100-150, a member who stays for 5 years saves you $500-750 in marketing.

**Referrals.** Members refer at 3-4x the rate of non-members. They're invested in your company and actively recommend you.

**Demand smoothing.** Maintenance visits are scheduled in your slow months. This keeps your techs busy and productive year-round instead of sitting idle in spring and fall.

## Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

**Pricing too low to "get people in the door."** A $49/year HVAC membership loses you $100+ per member per year in delivery costs. You'll build a huge member base that drains your profitability.

**Not including enough value.** If the membership only includes a tune-up and nothing else, there's no compelling reason to commit. Add priority scheduling, discounts, and waived fees to make it sticky.

**Discounting the membership itself.** Don't run "50% off your first year" promotions. You'll attract price shoppers who cancel at renewal. Instead, add a bonus (free filter upgrade, free indoor air quality test) to make the offer compelling without devaluing the price.

**Forgetting to raise prices.** Membership prices should increase 3-5% annually, just like your service rates. Grandfather existing members for one renewal cycle, then move them to the new rate.
