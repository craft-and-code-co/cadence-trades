# How to Sell Memberships in the Field — Scripts, Incentives, and Objection Handling

Your technicians are your best salespeople for memberships — they're in the customer's home, they've just solved a problem, and they have the customer's trust. But most techs weren't hired to sell, and they'll resist anything that feels "salesy." The key is giving them a natural way to present the membership as a benefit, not a pitch.

## When to Present the Membership

**After the repair, before the invoice.** The ideal moment is right after you've completed the work and the customer is happy. They've just seen your professionalism, and they're relieved the problem is fixed. This is peak trust.

**During the diagnostic, when explaining the repair.** If you're presenting Good/Better/Best options, the Best option should include a membership. "The Best option includes our Preferred Maintenance Plan, which gives you two tune-ups a year, priority scheduling, and 20% off any future repairs."

**During a maintenance visit.** If they called for a tune-up but aren't a member, they're already sold on the concept of maintenance. Converting them is easy: "You're already paying $149 for this visit. For $219/year, you'd get this visit plus a second tune-up, priority scheduling, and 20% off all repairs. Want me to set that up?"

## The Script Framework

Techs don't need a word-for-word script — that sounds robotic. They need a framework they can deliver naturally in their own voice.

### The Three-Step Presentation

**Step 1: Plant the seed (during the visit)**
"By the way, do you have a maintenance plan with anyone for your [HVAC system / plumbing / electrical]?"

Most will say no. Now you've opened the conversation without a hard pitch.

**Step 2: Explain the value (after the repair)**
"We have a maintenance program that a lot of our customers use. For [$X/month or $X/year], you'd get [two tune-ups a year], priority scheduling so you never wait more than 24 hours for service, and [20%] off all repairs. Based on today's repair alone, that discount would have saved you [$X]."

**Step 3: Make it easy to say yes**
"I can set it up right now on the tablet — takes about 30 seconds. Your first tune-up would be scheduled for [next month/season]. Want me to go ahead and get you set up?"

### Example: Post-Repair HVAC

"Mr. Johnson, your capacitor is replaced and everything's running great. Quick question — do you have a maintenance plan for your system? No? I'd recommend our Gold Plan. It's $19.99 a month and includes two full tune-ups a year — spring and fall — plus you'd get priority scheduling and 20% off repairs. Today's repair was $387 — with the membership, it would have been $310. The plan pays for itself on the first repair. Want me to get you signed up? Takes about 30 seconds."

## Handling Objections

### "I need to think about it."

"Totally understand. Here's what I'd suggest — I can sign you up today and your first payment won't process for 30 days. That gives you a full month to decide. If you change your mind, just call and cancel before then, no charge. But this way you're locked in at today's rate and your first tune-up is already scheduled."

### "It's too expensive."

"I hear you. Let me put it this way — the two tune-ups alone are worth $298 if you booked them separately. The membership is $219 for the year, so you're saving $79 just on the tune-ups. Everything else — the priority scheduling, the repair discounts, no overtime charges — that's all free on top. It's actually the cheapest way to maintain your system."

### "I'll just call you when I need something."

"You absolutely can, and we'd be happy to help. The difference is that without the plan, you're in our regular queue — which in the summer can be 3-5 days out. Members get next-day service, guaranteed. And most of the issues we find on tune-ups are things you'd never notice until they cause a breakdown. The tune-up is what prevents the emergency call."

### "I already have a home warranty."

"A home warranty covers breakdowns, but it doesn't cover maintenance — and most manufacturers require annual maintenance to keep your equipment warranty valid. Our plan handles the maintenance side, and if something does break, the repair discount saves you money on your deductible or out-of-pocket costs."

### "My spouse needs to approve it."

"Completely understand. I'll leave this info sheet with all the details. If you'd like, I can follow up with a text or email in a couple of days so you can discuss it together. What works better?"

## Tech Incentives

Techs will sell memberships consistently if they're compensated for it.

- **$25-50 per membership sold** — paid on next paycheck (immediate is better than monthly)
- **Tiered bonus:** 5-9 memberships/month = $35 each; 10+ = $50 each (retroactive)
- **Monthly contest:** Top membership seller gets a $200 bonus or a gift card
- **Renewal credit:** $10-15 per renewal for customers your tech originally signed up

**Track it visibly.** Post a leaderboard in the shop. Recognition drives behavior as much as cash does.

## Equipping Your Techs

**Tablet or printed leave-behind.** Give techs a one-page comparison showing what's included at each tier and the savings vs. retail pricing. Visual aids increase conversion by 30-40%.

**Role-play in meetings.** Spend 10 minutes in your weekly team meeting having techs practice the presentation on each other. Comfort comes from repetition, not from reading a script once.

**Don't force it.** Make it clear that every customer should hear about the membership, but the tech should never pressure. Present, explain, offer — and if the customer says no, thank them and move on. Pushy techs create bad reviews.

## Conversion Rate Targets

- **Good:** 15-20% of eligible service calls result in a membership sign-up
- **Great:** 25-35%
- **Elite:** 35-45%

An "eligible" call is any service or repair call where the customer is not already a member. If your techs run 8 eligible calls per day and convert at 25%, that's 2 memberships per tech per day — 10 per week, 40 per month per tech. At $200/year average membership value, that's $8,000/month in new recurring revenue per tech. Scale that across your team and you'll build a membership base that transforms your business.
