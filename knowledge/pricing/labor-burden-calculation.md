# The True Cost of an Employee — Labor Burden Calculation

Here's the number one mistake trades business owners make with pricing: they think a tech who earns $25/hr costs them $25/hr. It doesn't. That tech's true burdened cost is $42-48/hr — nearly double their wage. If you're pricing jobs based on the hourly wage alone, you're losing money on every single call and you don't even know it.

## What Is Labor Burden?

Labor burden is everything you pay on top of an employee's base wage. It includes mandatory costs (taxes, insurance) and operational costs (vehicle, tools, training) that are directly tied to having that person on your payroll.

## The Full Burden Breakdown

Let's use a real example: an HVAC service tech earning $28/hr ($58,240/year).

### Mandatory Employer Costs

| Item | Rate | Annual Cost |
|------|------|-------------|
| FICA (Social Security + Medicare) | 7.65% | $4,455 |
| Federal Unemployment (FUTA) | 0.6% | $350 |
| State Unemployment (SUTA) | 2.5% (varies) | $1,456 |
| Workers' Compensation | 5-12% (trades avg 8%) | $4,659 |

**Subtotal mandatory:** ~$10,920/year = **$5.25/hr**

### Benefits

| Item | Annual Cost |
|------|-------------|
| Health insurance (employer share) | $7,200-$12,000 |
| Dental/vision | $1,200 |
| Retirement match (3%) | $1,747 |
| Paid holidays (7 days) | $1,568 |
| Paid vacation (10 days) | $2,240 |
| Paid sick days (5 days) | $1,120 |

**Subtotal benefits:** ~$15,075-$19,875/year = **$7.25-$9.55/hr**

### Operational Costs (Per Tech)

| Item | Annual Cost |
|------|-------------|
| Vehicle (payment + insurance + fuel + maintenance) | $12,000-$18,000 |
| Tools and equipment | $2,000-$4,000 |
| Uniforms | $500-$1,000 |
| Phone/tablet | $1,200 |
| Training and certification | $1,500-$3,000 |
| GPS/fleet tracking | $300-$600 |

**Subtotal operational:** ~$17,500-$27,800/year = **$8.40-$13.35/hr**

## The Total Picture

| Component | Hourly Cost |
|-----------|-------------|
| Base wage | $28.00 |
| Mandatory taxes/insurance | $5.25 |
| Benefits | $7.25-$9.55 |
| Operational costs | $8.40-$13.35 |
| **Total Burdened Rate** | **$48.90-$56.15** |

That $28/hr tech actually costs you **$49-56/hr**. The burden multiplier is typically 1.7x to 2.0x the base wage.

## The Quick Formula

If you don't want to calculate every line item, use these multipliers:

- **No benefits, basic truck:** 1.5x base wage
- **Standard benefits package:** 1.75x base wage
- **Full benefits + nice truck + training:** 2.0x base wage

So a $25/hr tech with standard benefits: $25 x 1.75 = **$43.75/hr** burdened cost.

## Why This Matters for Pricing

If your burdened labor rate is $48/hr and a repair takes 1.5 hours average, your labor cost for that job is $72 — not the $42 you'd calculate using just the base wage. That $30 difference per job, multiplied across 8-12 calls per day, is $240-$360 per day in unrecovered costs. That's $60,000-$90,000 per year per tech that you're giving away.

## Billable Hours Reality Check

Here's another painful truth: your techs don't bill 8 hours per day. Between drive time, callbacks, training days, meetings, and slow days, the average service tech bills 5-6 productive hours per day. That means your effective burdened rate is even higher:

- $48/hr burdened / 5.5 billable hours x 8 paid hours = **$69.80/effective billable hour**

Your price book needs to recover that $69.80/hr — not $28/hr — or you're subsidizing every job out of your own pocket.

## Action Steps

1. Calculate your actual burdened rate for each tech (or use the average)
2. Plug that number into your price book formulas
3. If your current prices were built on base wage, you need an immediate price increase
4. Recalculate every 12 months as insurance, wages, and costs change
5. Use burdened rate when evaluating whether to hire — you need enough revenue to cover the full cost, not just the paycheck
